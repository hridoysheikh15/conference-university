About The Conference

Advancing Physics Research Through Global Collaboration

The International Conference on Physics 2026 (ICP 2026) is organized by the Physics Discipline, Khulna University, to create a platform where researchers, academics, and students can exchange ideas, present original research, and build lasting collaborations across the physical sciences.

Our Mission

Foster interdisciplinary dialogue that accelerates discovery in modern physics.

Our Objective

Connect emerging researchers with established scientists from partner institutions.

Who Attends

Faculty, postgraduate researchers, and industry scientists from 20+ countries.

Recognition

Best paper and best poster awards presented across every research track.

Research Tracks

Conference Topics

Submit your original research across sixteen specialised tracks in physics and applied sciences.

Quantum Physics

Quantum computing, entanglement, and foundational quantum theory research.

Nuclear Physics

Nuclear structure, reactions, radiation detection and applications.

Astrophysics

Stellar evolution, cosmology, gravitational waves and observational astronomy.

Particle Physics

Standard model tests, collider physics and beyond-standard-model searches.

Plasma Physics

Fusion research, space plasmas and plasma-based technologies.

Computational Physics

Numerical modelling, simulations and high-performance computing methods.

Optics

Classical and quantum optics, imaging systems and optical instrumentation.

Photonics

Lasers, fiber optics, photonic devices and integrated photonics.

Nanotechnology

Nanomaterials, nanofabrication and nanoscale device engineering.

Condensed Matter

Solid-state physics, superconductivity and low-dimensional systems.

Medical Physics

Radiotherapy, medical imaging and diagnostic instrumentation research.

Renewable Energy Physics

Solar cells, energy storage and sustainable power generation.

High Energy Physics

Collider experiments, cosmic rays and high-energy phenomena.

Materials Science

Novel materials characterization, synthesis and functional properties.

Theoretical Physics

Mathematical physics, field theory and foundational frameworks.

Scientific Computing

Data-driven physics, machine learning applications and big-data analysis.
